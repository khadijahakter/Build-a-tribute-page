Althea Gibson

One of the first Black athletes to cross the color line of international tennis

Althea Gibson, in action at Wimbledon in 1957.

Here's a timeline of Gibson's life:

  • 1927 - Born Aug. 25 in Silver, S.C.
  • 1930 - Moves to Harlem district in New York City.
  • 1942 - Enters and wins her first tournament, sponsored by the all-black American Tennis Association (ATA).
  • 1946 - Moves to Wilmington, N.C., to work on her tennis game with Dr. Hubert A. Eaton; enrolls in high school.
  • 1947 - Wins the first of 10 consecutive ATA National Championships.
  • 1949 - Competes against white players for the first time; enrolls at Florida A&M University in Tallahassee, Fla.
  • 1950 - Graduates from Florida A&M; moves to Jefferson City, Mo.
  • 1951 - Starts working with tennis coach Sydney Llewellyn.
  • 1952 - Travels throughout Southeast Asia on a U.S. State Department-sponsored goodwill tennis tour.
  • 1953 - Becomes the first African-American to win the French Championships; wins the first of three consecutive Wimbledon women's doubles titles, each time with a different partner; tours the Australian tennis circuit.

“In sports, you simply aren't considered a real champion until you have defended your title successfully. Winning it once can be a fluke; winning it twice proves you are the best.”

-- Althea Neale Gibson, American tennis player and professional golfer
